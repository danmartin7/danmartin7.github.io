CWU Writing Program History

The Washington Normal School (1891)

Central Washington College of Education (1937)

Central Washington State College (1961)

Central Washington University (1977)

  • 1889: Washington is admitted to the Union as a state on November 11th. 
  • 1890: The Washington State Normal School at Ellensburg was established by an Act from the Legislature, approved March 28. The first legislature in 1890 enacted a law that read, ''There shall be established in the City of Ellensburg, County of Kittitas, a school for the training and education of teachers in the art of instructing and governing in the public schools of the State." 
  • 1891: The Washington State Normal School at Ellensburg opened on September 17. I've seen the 6th of September listed as an opening-date, too. 
  • 1892-93: There is no one listed for the English department or as faculty for teaching writing courses this year, but Elvira Marquis, an English teacher listed in upcoming years, is listed in the 1904-05 catalogue as a teacher at the Normal school from 1892-97.
  • 1893: The main building for the school is completed. It's Barge now.  
  • 1893-94: Elvira Marquis is listed as the first faculty member for the English department at the Normal School.
  • 1894-95: There is no catalogue covering these years at the Normal School. We can assume Marquis is teaching English courses this year.
  • 1895-96: Elvira Marquis is listed as English faculty and as a secretary to the Principal for 1896-97.
  • 1896-97: There is no catalogue for this year, but Marquis is listed for English in the 1895-96 catalogue for these years.
  • 1897-98: There is no catalogue accounting for this year. We know Marquis retired in 1897, so it unlikely she taught courses in English this year or for the entire year. There is a transition in leadership taking place for writing and English during this year and the following year. It is unknown who exactly is teaching English or running the English department this year. 
  • 1898-99: There is a faculty page in the catalogue but no one is listed for English for the 1899-1900 year. English is the only department missing a faculty page. This is probably the year Marquis left or retired from Washington State Normal School and someone else was brought in to takeover. 
  • 1899-1900: Laura Grace Riddell is identified as faculty for the English Language, Literature department
  • 1900-1901: Laura Grace Riddell is from Stanford. She is listed for faculty in English Literature and Language for this year, too.
  • 1901-1902: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris takes over the English department. She has a PhD from Yale. She brings the Yale school theory to Washington. Riddell retires in 1902. It is noted in the 1910-11 catalogue that Riddell is in Forestry service in Portland, OR. 
  • 1902-1903: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is listed as faculty for the English department. Harris is still in charge of The English Language and Literature but there is an additional person this year, Bethesda I. Beals, Ph. B., who teaches Latin and English. This looks like the first hiring of an instructor to teach non literature courses. 
  • 1903-1904: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris for Literature and Bethesda Beals for Latin and English. 
  • 1904-1905: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris for The English Language, Literature and Bethesda Beals for Latin and English.
  • 1905-1906 :Dr. Ella Isabel Harris for The English Language, Literature and Bethesda Beals for Latin and English.
  • 1906-1907: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris for The English Language, Literature and Bethesda Beals for Latin and English. An appropriation of $75,000 for additional buildings was made by the Tenth Legislature and approved March 15, 1907. 
  • 1907 Quarterly: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris was an editor of this Quarterly. Beals retires and is replaced with Miss Alberta MacDonnell, A. B., a graduate of the Washington Normal School in the class of 1899, who since then has studied for two years at Vassar College, one year at Stanford University, and one year at the University of Michigan, graduating in 1906 from Michigan. It is noted in the 1910-11 catalogue that Beals is now a secretary for the Washington Society for the Prevention and Relief of Tuberculosis in Seattle, Washington. 
  • 1907-1908 :Dr. Ella Isabel Harris and Alberta MacDonnell are teaching English courses. MacDonnell is listed on the faculty page as an Assistant. 
  • : Dr. Ella Isabel Harris and Alberta MacDonnell are teaching English courses. 
  • 1909-1910: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris and Alberta MacDonnell are teaching English courses. 
  • 1910-1911: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is still listed as faculty for English but MacDonnell is not listed. Blanche M Hazelton, A.B. is listed as an Assistant in English and Latin now. MacDonnell is noted as a teacher in Tacoma now. 
  • 1911-1912: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris and Blanche Hazelton are teaching English courses.
  • 1912-1913: Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is still listed as faculty for English but Hazelton is gone. >Florence Wilson, B. A., is now listed an Assistant in English and Florence Bullock is listed for History and English. Wilson is listed with Harris on the English Department course outline. Harris has been at the Normal School now for 11 years and she has built a robust department and course offering. 
  • 1913-1914:  Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is still listed as faculty for English and Florence Wilson is listed as an Assistant. 
  • 1914-1915:  Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is still listed as faculty for English and Florence Wilson is listed as an Assistant.
  • 1915-1916:  Dr. Ella Isabel Harris is still listed as faculty for English and Florence Wilson is no longer listed as an Assistant. She is listed as English like Harris. This will be Harris's last year. Harris passed away in 1921. Wilson went to UCLA to the Textile department. 
  • 1916-1917: Margaret Adair Davidson is now an Assistant in the English Department with Wilson. Davidson Graduated from Emerson College of Oratory, Boston. She is a graduate of the Washington State Normal School. Harris retired after 15 years at the Normal School. Things really start to change now. Major changes occur this year and quite a bit of Harris's work is dismantled. There are new courses and the courses are designed with teacher in mind. 
  • 1917-1918: Mr. Selden Smyser is now the Head of the Department of Social Science and English, and Wilson is an Assistant in English again. Smyser is working as an interim. Selden Smyser has a Ph. B., from De Pauw University; Fellow in Economics; M. A., Ohio State University; Graduate Student, Cornell University. Margaret Adair Davidson is also listed as an Assistant in the English Department. A Eugene Ormsbee is also listed as an Assistant in Social Sciences and English. Smyser is a sociologist. Davidson is listed as an Assistant to English in the Summer of 1918. Hazel Berry is the Assistant to English at the Centralia campus during the summer of the same year. LILLIAN SYLVESTER is listed as a Supervisor of Writing for Centralia in 1918. She is from Yakima Public Schools in the Writing and Art department. 
  • 1918-1919: Nicholas E. Hinch is now the Head of the Department of English and Modern Languages. He is a Graduate of Ontario Normal College; A. B., Toronto University; Graduate Student University of Chicago, Harvard University and Columbia University. Davidson is still listed as an Assistant. 
  • 1919-1920: Nicholas E. Hinch (Head of English Department) and Margaret Davidson (Assistant) are teaching English courses. Hinch is able to establish ethos from being a graduate student but not actually earning a graduate degree. 
  • 1920-1921: Nicholas E. Hinch (Head of English Department) and Margaret Davidson (Assistant) are teaching English courses.
  • 1921-1922: Hinch is listed as English and Modern Language and Davidson is listed as English and Expression. There is no head or assistant labels for the summer catalogues but there is for the main catalogue. Mabel Lytton and Alice Wilmarth are listed as Assistants to English for the summer of 1922. 
  • 1923-1924: Hinch and Davidson are listed for English.
  • 1924-1925: Hinch and Davidson are listed for English. Marie Jeanne Teisseire, A. B., A. M., is listed now as an Assistant in Department of English and Foreign Languages. 
  • 1925-1926: Hinch and Davidson are listed for English. Jean McMorran, A. B., A. M., is listed as an Assistant to the Department of English. 
  • 1926-1927: Hinch and Davidson are listed for English
  • 1927-1928: Hinch is still listed as department head for English but Davidson is gone and John W Wright, B.A., is listed as an Assistant in Literature and Director of Department of Dramatic Art. Hinch has been running the English department for 10 years at this point. The department is also listed as English, Literature, and Allied Arts.
  • 1928-1929: This year appears to be woven into the next catalogue (1929-1930). 
  • 1929-1930: It is now the ENGLISH AND FOREIGN LANGUAGE and LITERATURE department and it is run by Jean McMorran. She is listed as the Director. Nicholas Hinch is listed as English Composition and Foreign Languages. France Frater is listed as Dramatic Interpretation and Speech: Iva M. Jones is listed as Part-time Teacher, Children's Literature.
  • 1930-1931: We have the same people in charge. Jean McMorran is on absence for the year but is director of the department. Nicholas Hinch is listed for English Composition. MARIE TESS AIRE SCHULLER is listed for Foreign Languages. Frances Fratrer is listed for Dramatic Interpretation and Speech. GRACE I. MACARTHUR is listed for Literature. Mary I. Simpson is listed for Children's Literature.
  • 1931-1932: MARGARET COFFIN is listed for ENGLISH AND SOCIAL SCIENCE. She has a B. A. from University of Washington and she is a Graduate (Diploma) of Ellensburg State Normal School. Jean McMorran is listed for ENGLISH. She has a B. A. from the University of Washington and a M. A. from Columbia University.
  • 1932-1933 : Jean McMorran is listed for ENGLISH. She is now a Graduate Student at the University of Washington and the University of Florence, Italy. Nicholas Hinch is listed as ENGLISH. He is now a Graduate Student at University of Washington and University College, Oxford. MARGARET COFFIN is listed for ENGLISH. She has a B. A. from the University of Washington and a Graduate Diploma from Ellensburg State Normal School. She is also a Graduate Student at the University of Vienna and Santa Barbara School of the Arts. EILEEN O'LEARY is listed for SPEECH AND DRAMATICS. She has a B. A. from the University of Washington and she is a Graduate Student at the University of Washington. MARY I. SIMPSON is listed for CHILDREN’S LITERATURE. She has a B. A. and an M. A. from Colorado State Teachers College.
  • 1933: Washington Normal School granted right to award bachelors of arts degrees.
  • 1933-1934 : The English and Dramatics department list Jean McMorran at the top. DONALD E. MACRAE is a new faculty listed in English. He has a B. A., Drake University and an M. A. and a Ph. D. from the State University of Iowa Nicholas Hinch is still listed as English. MARGARET COFFIN Holmes is listed for English. AGNES HoWE is listed for Speech and Dramatics. She has a B. A. from Albion College and a M. A. from Northwestern University. *EILEEN O'LEARY is listed for Speech and Dramatics. She has a B. A. from the University of Washington and she is a Graduate Student at the University of Washington. MARY I. SIMPSON is listed again for Children's Literature.
  • 1934-1935: McMorran, Hinch, O'Leary, and Simpson are listed as English faculty for the summer of 1935.
  • 1935-1936: DONALD E . MACRAE and Hinch are listed as an Associate Professor of English now. Russel W. Lembke is listed as an Assistant Professor of Speech and Dramatics. He has a B. A. and an M.A. from State University of Iowa. Andrew Mathews is listed as an Instructor in French and English. He has an A. B. and an M. A. from the University of Georgia. He is a Graduate Student from the University of Oregon. MARY I. SIMPS0N is listed as an lnstructor of Children 's Literature. 
  • 1936-1937: Donald E. MacRae, Ph.D is listed in English for summer with Lembke and Hinch. It is the Languages, Literature and Speech department, so this might be where the Language and Literature building name comes from.
  • 1937-1938: The Normal School became Central Washington College of Education. MacRae, Lembke, Hinch, Simpson, and Mathews are still working in the Language and Literature department.  CATHERINE BULLARD is listed as an Instructor in English now. She has an A. B . from Rio Grande College and a B. S. and M. A. from the University of Minnesota. MacRae is now a full professor in the Language and Literature department. 
  • 1938-1939: Donald MacRae, Lembke, Hinch, Simpson, Bullard, and Mathews are still working in the Language and Literature department. CATHERINE BULLARD is listed as an Assistant Professor now. We will find out later that Bullard is the most published of all of these professors. 
  • 1939-1940: MacRae, Bullard, Hinch Lembke, and Mathews are still teaching in Language and Literature. There are two new faculty. OLIVER W. NELSON is listed as an Instructor in Speech. He has a B. A. and an M. A. from the University of Washington. There is also a name handwritten at the top: Graham Dressler. He has a PhD but the handwriting is hard to decipher so I don't know where the degree was earned. 
  • 1940-1941: MacRae, Bullard, Hinch, Lembke, Nelson, and Mathews
  • 1941-1942: MacRae, Bullard, Hinch, Lembke, Nelson, and Mathews
  • 1942-1943: MacRae, Bullard, Hinch, Lembke, Nelson, and Mathews. ANNETTE H. HITCHCOCK is listed as a new Instructor in English. She has a B. A. from the University of North Dakota and a M. A . from Teachers College at Columbia University. Mathews is an Associate Professor now. Remember that Bullard will have the most publications of any faculty in the English department ever here at CWC by the end of this decade.
  • 1943-1944: CATHARINE BULLARD is now listed as an Associate Professor of English and she now has a Ph. D. from the University of Minnesota. She is listed at the top of the department. CECILIA CUTTS is listed as an Assistant Professor of Spanish and French for Spanish and French Acting. She has an A . B., A. M., and Ph. D from the University of Washington. ANNETTE H. HITCHCOCK is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. She has a B. A. from the University of North Dakota and an M . A. from the Teachers College, Columbia University. LEMBKE is still listed as an Associate Professor of Speech and Dramatics; B. A ., M . A . OLIVER W . NELSON is listed as an Speech Associate Professor of Speech. In another catalogue for the same year, Hinch, MacRae, and Mathews are listed for the department with all of these other faculty except Cutts. Honestly, keeping up with all the faculty listings in each catalogue and the inconsistencies with the listings has been hard. I'm not sure why they included or excluded certain names in certain catalogues even when they were still in the department. For the Summer of 1943, Mary C. Richards is listed as an Acting Instructor in English. She has a A. B. from Reed College and a M. A. and Ph. D. from the University of California.
  • 1944 Summer: Catharine Bullard is listed as an Associate Professor of English. Annette H. Hitchcock is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. Russell W. Lembke is listed as an Associate Professor of Speech and Dramatics. Lyman M. Partridge is listed as a Visiting Instructor in Speech. She has a B. A. from BYU, an M. A. from Columbia University and a Ph. D from the University of Michigan.  
  • 1944-1945: Bullard, Lembke, Hitchcock, MacRae, and Mathews are listed for Language and Literature. Hinch is listed as en Emeritus for 1943 in this catalogue.
  • 1945-1946 : DONALD E. McRAE is listed as a Professor of English. CATHARINE BULLARD is listed as a Professor of English. ELLA MAI CLARK is listed as an Acting Instructor in French and Spanish. She has a B.A. from Vanderbilt University and an M.A. from the University of Illinois. She is a graduate Student at the University of Minnesota, University of Illinois, and Northwestern Downtown College. ANNETTE HITCHCOCK is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. Russell W. LEMBKE is listed as an Associate Professor of Speech and Dramatics. Andrew J. MATHEWS is listed as an Associate Professor of French and English. LYMAN A. PARTRIDGE is listed as an Associate Professor of Speech with an A.B. from Brigham Young University and an M.A. from Columbia University and a Ph.D. from the University of Michigan. Ruth Slonim is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. She has a B.E. from Duluth State Teachers College and an M.A. from the University of Minnesota. She is a Graduate Student at the University of Minnesota and the State University of Iowa. Lembke and Mathews are listed as away at war or doing war work.
  • 1946-1947 : CATHARINE BULLARD is listed as Professor of English. ANNETTE HITCHCOCK is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. LYMAN A. PARTRIDGE is listed as an Associate Professor of Speech. Ruth Slonim is listed as an Assistant Professor of English. DENNIS G. HANNAN is listed as an Instructor in French and Spanish with a B.A. from the University of Washington. He’s also a Graduate Student at the University of Washington. NORMAN S. HOWELL is listed as an Assistant Professor of Speech and Dramatics with a B.A. and M.A. from State College of Washington. He is also a Graduate Student at the University of Washington.
  • 1946: Summer: Catharine Bullard is listed as an English Professor of English. She has a Ph.D. from the University of Minnesota. Ella Mai Clark, Annette H. Hitchcock, Norman S. Howell, Lyman M. Partridge.
  • 1947: “The 1947 Legislature authorized the Colleges of Education to grant the Degree of Master of Education in Elementary Education. The Central Washington College will begin graduate courses leading to the above degree during the Summer Session of 1947” (p. 9). There is also a note that “Beginning with the Summer Session of 1947 the Central Washington College will offer courses leading to the Bachelor of Arts [and Sciences] Degree[s] in several fields in addition to Education” (p. 9).  
  • 1947-1948: CATHERINE BULLARD is listed as a Professor of English. HAZEL BRAIN is listed as an Assistant Professor of English with a B.A. from Central Washington College of Education and an M.A. from Northwestern University and a Graduate Student at Maholy Nagy School or Design, Chicago. Norman S. Howell is listed as an Assistant Professor of Speech and Dramatics. LYMAN M. PARTRIDGE is listed as an Associate Professor of Speech. JACQUELINE MARTIN is listed as an Instructor in French and Spanish.
  • 1948-1949: CATHARINE BULLARD, Professor of English. HAZEL BRAIN, Assistant Professor of English. BERT C. CROSS, instructor of journalism. Sidnie M. DAVIES, Assistant Professor of English. DENNIS G. HANNAN, Assistant Professor. ANNETTE HITCHCOCK, Assistant Professor. NORMANS. HOWELL, Assistant Professor of Speech and Dramatics. HJALMAR 0. LOKENSGARD, Assistant Professor of English. Roy P. LUDTKE, Assistant Professor. LYMA M. PARTRIDGE, Associate Professor of Speech.

Sidnie M. DAVIES, Assistant Professor of English. ODETTE GOLDEN is listed as an Instructor in French and Spanish B.A., Barnard College; M.A., Teachers College, Columbia University. MATHEWSON is listed as an Instructor in English A .B., Smith College; M.A., University of Colorado; Graduate Student, Northwestern University.
